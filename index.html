Just a little bit about my favorite hobby:
Nail Art!

Nails are Amazing

Ever since I was little I loved nail art. My obsession began when my aunt gave me a kaboodle filled with her old nail polish. Needless to say, I was hooked. After running out of space on my own fingers and toes, I recruited my female family members to aid in my budding artistry. I got to practice laying the polish down smoothly while they got a free manicure. A definite win-win! By the time I got into college I had quite the nail art collection. It began as mostly polishes of various colors and finishes; some were satiny while others were bright and sparkly and eventually progressed to include rhinestones, stickers, and small clay flowers.
My roommate and I would play around with different designs to decompress between exams and study halls. Needless to say, our nails stayed on point all throughout our matriculation lol.

Off to Beauty School

After graduation I moved to Tampa to be closer to my grandmother. While trying to figure out if I truly wanted togo to law school, I decided to enroll in a local beauty school. I figured my time deciding should be spent doing something that was both productive and enjoyable. Plus, with a nail tech certification, I could always work freelance. So away I went to Manhattan Beauty Academy as a fledgling nail tech.
Once enrolled, I was given my first acrylic nail kit. It included files, a brush, and the liquid and powders to sculpt acrylic nails. Having only used nail polish, it took some time to get accustomed to working with acrylic.

Perfecting the Craft

Once I got the acrylic thing down I started following nail tutorials on youtube to learn new techniques. My mind was blown at all the different effects one could do with paint, acrylic, embellishments, and gels. I had to learn them all. Roses are one of my favorite things to make with acrylic, so I practiced sculpting them between clients. With time, acrylic sculpting became my specialty. Many of my clients were sweet enough to let me use their nails to practice on. I'd give them flowers, swirls, and bows, sometimes little characters.

Since beauty school, my nail collection has grown from that humble kaboodle to include multicolored acrylics and gels, alloy and gem embellishments, way too many nail polishes to count along with stickers, foils, holographic flake, stamps and pigment powders. Unfortunately, I don't do nails professionally anymore but I"ll occasionally bust out the kit when the mood strikes. Again I say, nails are awesome. They helped me make some great friends and memories that I'll remember fondly. It was such a great experience learning and doing something that I loved and I get the feeling my time with Launch Code will be just as fulfilling.
Thanks for reading!
